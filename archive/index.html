our love story.



Our story began in high school, in the most unexpected of places: Chinese class. Peter strolled into class, a bit late, of course, and instantly felt his heart race. Oh no, a test! Spotting Vicki, the smartest girl in class, he dashed over. “Hey, can I peek at your paper to double-check my answers?” he whispered, giving her his best puppy-dog eyes. Vicki rolled her eyes but could not help but smile. “Fine, but just this once!”

After class, they never talked again and life moved on. Fast forward two years: Vicki and Peter found themselves with 9th period free, and fate had a little mischief planned. A mutual friend teased them about having feelings for each other, sparking laughter and chats that stretched late into the night—keeping sleepy Taurus Vicki up past her usual 10 PM bedtime!

Those all nighters? Well, they sparked something special, and before they knew it, Peter took Vicki out on a date to Skyview Mall in Flushing where they played the game: 20 questions. When the date got to the end, Peter's 20th question was two-fold. He turned to Vicki and asked "Vicki, would you be my prom date?". Vicki smiled and nodded yes. Her answer ignited a fire in him so then Peter summoned up the courage and double downed on his ask, "Vicki, would you like to be my girlfriend?". Vicki once again smiled and nodded yes.

And now, here they are, ready to start the next chapter of their lives together. It only took 13 years.

travel & stay.


Transportation

The wedding venue is located about 3 hours from Taipei, but no need to worry about the journey—we're offering complimentary transportation from Taipei to Sun Moon Lake on November 23rd. We highly recommend arriving in Taipei before then to take advantage of this service.

If you prefer to travel independently, the venue is also accessible via the high-speed rail, followed by a scenic 1.5-hour car ride. As the big day approaches, we’ll be in touch to coordinate your travel plans and make sure everything goes smoothly!


Accommodations

The wedding party will be staying at Deer Chaser, where the afterparty will also take place, and we highly recommend booking your stay here to keep the celebration going! If this doesn’t work for you, no worries—we’ve curated a few nearby accommodations for your convenience:

- Hotelday Sun Moon Lake

- Blue Sky Bay B&B

Please note that while we'd love to accommodate everyone, transportation to and from the wedding venue will only be available for guests staying at these locations. We hope this helps with your planning!

Costs & Payments

Do I need to pay for my own accomodations and travels?

We kindly ask our guests to arrange their own accommodations and flights. To make your travel experience more seamless, we will be providing complimentary roundtrip transportation from Taipei to Sun Moon Lake. Please find more information on the Travel & Stay page.



Will there be any expenses during the wedding (e.g., cash bar, tips)?

Tipping at weddings in Taiwan is not necessary. We will cover all the service & tipping fee at the wedding.



Do I need to bring a gift or red bag?

We genuinely mean it when we say that your presence is the greatest gift we could ask for. We do not expect any additional presents. Destination weddings require a significant commitment, and we are deeply grateful for the effort you’ve made to celebrate with us.



Taiwan

When should I arrive?

Travelling can be quite stressful especially on your body. Kindly note, Taiwan has a 12 hour time zone difference so we recommend you fly in a few days before 11/23/2025 to rest up before joining us for our wedding festivities.



Do I need to bring cash, or are credit cards widely accepted at the destination?

Cash is king in Taiwan, and the most convenient place to exchange it is at Taoyuan Airport. While banks in Taiwan do offer currency exchange services, the process can often be cumbersome. Credit cards are widely accepted, particularly in larger cities like Taipei, and digital payment systems such as Apple Pay and LINE Pay are gaining popularity. However, it's still wise to carry some cash, especially when visiting smaller towns or rural areas where electronic payments may be less common.



Do I need a visa to enter Taiwan?

You do not need a visa to enter Taiwan.
